The Comstock Laws

Federal laws passed in 1873

The following were banned from the Postal Service

  • Obscenity
  • Contraceptives
  • Anything sexual

Additional Laws Went Even Further

Made it illegal to distribute any information about or dealing with abortion, obscenity, or contraception

  • Some anatomy textbooks weren't sent to medical students as they were judged obscene

Objective of the laws

  • Make premarital sex more risky
  • Suppress vices in the world through denial
  • Protect the morals of the youth

Definition of Obscenity

Material encouraging or engaging in sexual activity without major importance

Justification

Corruption in the youth and the home is due to obscene literature

Obscenity may corrupt marriage as well as the power of the church

Mainly focused on priviledged families support

One Congressman's rationale was that without the risk of pregnancy, sex would seem like a harmless activity
